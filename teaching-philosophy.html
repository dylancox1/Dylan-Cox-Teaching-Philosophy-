My Teaching Philosophy

Theoretical Approach

Social constructivist theory is utilised in the classroom, to transform the pedagogy of learning and education from a transactional relationship between teacher and student, to a more collaborative system that emphasises learning as a shared and collective responsibility. This develops problem solving skills through group collaboration and through experiencing different student inputs and perspectives, from multiple points of view. A much more pragmatic approach that is well suited to project based learning.

Project Based Learning

It's all in the name "Technology and Applied Studies", students learn about technologies through applying themselves to meaningful and pragatic outcomes. The design and production of projects is the best place to start, giving students the opportunity to undertake the design process from beginning to end. Making use of goal-orientated assessable projects allows access to higher order thinking skills from the get-go, encouraging and rewarding learning incrementally, especially when students have more input and control over their designs and projects.
